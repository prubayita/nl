Rwanda

Home of Mountain Gorillas

Discover Rwanda – the "land of a thousand hills" – where lush volcanoes, dense rainforests, and serene lakes set the scene for once-in-a-lifetime wildlife encounters. From gorilla trekking in Volcanoes National Park to chimpanzee tracking in Nyungwe and Big Five safaris in Akagera, Rwanda offers breathtaking nature, luxury lodges, and rich culture all year round.

Volcanoes National Park

Home to mountain gorillas and golden monkeys. Ideal for hiking, conservation experiences, and luxury trekking.

Nyungwe National Park

Explore ancient rainforests, spot chimpanzees and over 700 bird species, and enjoy guided nature walks.

Akagera National Park

A classic savannah safari destination to see the Big Five, with opportunities for birding and game drives.

Whether you seek thrilling wildlife encounters or peaceful lakeside retreats, Rwanda promises a deeply moving experience. Best visited from Dec–Feb and Jun–Sept.

Best Time to Visit Rwanda

Discover when to go for the best gorilla trekking, safaris, and cultural experiences.

June – September

High: 27°C / 80°F | Low: 16°C / 61°F | Rainfall: 33mm

  • Peak dry season — ideal for gorilla trekking.
  • Best hiking conditions with clear trails.
  • Lower malaria risk and lush, open scenery.

October – January

High: 26.5°C / 80°F | Low: 16°C / 61°F | Rainfall: 93mm

  • Short rainy season (Oct–Nov), drier again by January.
  • Gorilla trekking still possible with fewer tourists.
  • Warm climate with bursts of sunshine between showers.

February – May

High: 26.5°C / 80°F | Low: 16°C / 61°F | Rainfall: 112mm

  • February offers great gorilla trekking but higher prices.
  • March–May: long rainy season—ideal for chimpanzee tracking.
  • Scenery is vibrant, and birdwatchers enjoy colorful breeding plumage in May.

Best overall time: June to September for gorillas. For chimpanzees, go during March–May or October–November. Combine with a Kenya or Tanzania safari for a complete experience.
